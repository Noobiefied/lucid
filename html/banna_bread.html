Banana Bread

Succulent shrimp cooked in fragrant garlic butter with a hint of lemon. This elegant dish comes together in under 15 minutes, making it perfect for busy weeknights or impressive dinner parties.

Source: TeeYvette

Ingredients

Ingredients for banana bread are as follows:

  • 3 very ripe bananas (the more brown spots, the better)
  • ⅓ cup unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar (or ½ cup if you prefer less sweet)
  • 1 large egg, beaten, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt (about ¼ teaspoon)
  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • Optional: ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Optional: ½ cup chopped walnuts or chocolate chips

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Preheat and prepare: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with butter or non-stick spray, or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.
  • Mash the bananas: In a large mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until they're mostly smooth with just a few small lumps. You should have about 1½ cups of mashed banana. The riper and more spotty your bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful your bread will be.
  • Mix in the butter: Stir the melted butter into the mashed bananas until well combined. The butter should be melted but not hot, or it might scramble the egg in the next step.
  • Add sugar, egg and vanilla: Mix in the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract. Stir until everything is well incorporated and the mixture is smooth.
  • Add the leavening: Sprinkle the baking soda and salt over the banana mixture and stir to combine. You might see the mixture fizz slightly—this is normal and shows the baking soda is activated.
  • Fold in the flour: Add the flour (and cinnamon if using) and gently fold it in with a wooden spoon or spatula. Stir just until no flour streaks remain—do not overmix! Overmixing develops the gluten and makes the bread tough and dense. A few small lumps are okay.
  • Add mix-ins: If using walnuts or chocolate chips, gently fold them into the batter.
  • Pour and bake: Pour the batter into your prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter a couple times to release any air bubbles.
  • Bake: Bake for 55-65 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs (but no wet batter). The top should be deep golden brown. If the top is browning too quickly but the center isn't done, tent it loosely with aluminum foil for the last 15-20 minutes of baking.
  • Cool: Remove the pan from the oven and let the banana bread cool in the pan for about 10-15 minutes. Then turn it out onto a wire rack to cool completely. This makes it easier to slice cleanly.
  • Slice and serve: Once cooled, slice and enjoy! Banana bread is delicious warm with a pat of butter, or at room temperature. It also makes excellent toast the next day.

Storage: Wrap cooled banana bread tightly in plastic wrap or store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 4 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. It also freezes beautifully for up to 3 months—wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and then aluminum foil before freezing.

TIP: Don't throw away those overripe bananas! When your bananas get too spotty to eat, peel them and freeze them in a freezer bag. When you're ready to make banana bread, just thaw them, they'll be perfect. For extra moisture, you can add a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt to the batter. For a fun twist, try swirling in some Nutella or peanut butter before baking.
