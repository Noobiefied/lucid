Simple Marinara Recipe

Forget the jarred stuff—this homemade marinara is incredibly easy and tastes like it simmered all day. Fresh garlic and herbs transform simple canned tomatoes into a sauce you'll want to put on everything.

Source: Donna Griffin

Ingredients

Ingredients for chocolate chip cookies are as follows:

  • 2 (28-ounce) cans whole peeled tomatoes or crushed tomatoes
  • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 5-6 cloves garlic, minced or thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil (or 4-5 fresh basil leaves)
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Prepare your tomatoes: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. This prevents sticking and ensures even baking.
  • Heat the oil: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt until well combined. Set this aside while you prepare the wet ingredients.
  • Sauté the garlic: In a large mixing bowl or the bowl of a stand mixer, beat the softened butter with both sugars on medium speed for about 3-4 minutes. The mixture should become light and fluffy with a pale color. This step is crucial for creating cookies with the right texture.
  • Add the tomatoes: Beat in the eggs one at a time, making sure each is fully incorporated before adding the next. Add the vanilla extract and mix until combined. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  • Season the sauce: Gradually add the flour mixture to the butter mixture, mixing on low speed just until no flour streaks remain. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make your cookies tough.
  • Simmer: Using a wooden spoon or spatula, gently fold in the chocolate chips (and nuts if using) until evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  • Adjust seasoning: Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto your prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading. For more uniform cookies, you can roll the dough into balls.
  • Serve: Bake for 9-11 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown but the centers still look slightly underbaked. They will continue to cook on the hot pan after removing from the oven.

TIP: For a silkier sauce, use an immersion blender to blend it partially or completely. You can also add a splash of heavy cream for a creamy tomato sauce, or simmer with Italian sausage or meatballs for a heartier version.
