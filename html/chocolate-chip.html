Classic Chocolate Chip Recipe

The ultimate comfort food that brings back childhood memories with every bite. These golden-brown cookies strike the perfect balance between crispy edges and a soft, gooey center packed with chocolate.

Source: Atalanya

Ingredients

Ingredients for chocolate chip cookies are as follows:

  • 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • Optional: 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Prepare your workspace: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats. This prevents sticking and ensures even baking.
  • Mix the dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt until well combined. Set this aside while you prepare the wet ingredients.
  • Cream the butter and sugars: In a large mixing bowl or the bowl of a stand mixer, beat the softened butter with both sugars on medium speed for about 3-4 minutes. The mixture should become light and fluffy with a pale color. This step is crucial for creating cookies with the right texture.
  • Add eggs and vanilla: Beat in the eggs one at a time, making sure each is fully incorporated before adding the next. Add the vanilla extract and mix until combined. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  • Combine wet and dry: Gradually add the flour mixture to the butter mixture, mixing on low speed just until no flour streaks remain. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make your cookies tough.
  • Fold in chocolate chips: Using a wooden spoon or spatula, gently fold in the chocolate chips (and nuts if using) until evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  • Shape the cookies: Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto your prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading. For more uniform cookies, you can roll the dough into balls.
  • Bake: Bake for 9-11 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown but the centers still look slightly underbaked. They will continue to cook on the hot pan after removing from the oven.
  • Cool: Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. This helps them set properly without breaking.

TIP: For chewier cookies, slightly underbake them. For crispier cookies, bake an extra 1-2 minutes. You can also chill the dough for 30 minutes before baking for thicker cookies that spread less.
