Simple Fried Rice

Transform leftover rice into a delicious one-pan meal packed with vegetables and savory flavors. Quick, customizable, and endlessly satisfying for lunch or dinner.

Source: ToppinKitchen

Ingredients

Ingredients for simple fried rice are as follows:

  • 3 cups cooked rice, preferably day-old and refrigerated
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn, diced bell peppers)
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 3 green onions, sliced (white and green parts separated)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional but recommended)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • ½ teaspoon white pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional additions: diced cooked chicken, shrimp, pork, or tofu

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Prepare the rice: The key to great fried rice is using cold, day-old rice. Fresh rice is too moist and will become mushy. If your rice is clumped together, break it apart with your hands or a fork before cooking. If you only have fresh rice, spread it on a baking sheet and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to dry it out.
  • Scramble the eggs: Heat a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and swirl to coat. Pour in the beaten eggs and let them sit for about 10 seconds, then scramble them, breaking them into small pieces. When just set but still slightly soft, transfer to a plate and set aside.
  • Cook the aromatics: Add another tablespoon of oil to the same wok. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and the white parts of the green onions. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  • Add vegetables: Add your mixed vegetables to the wok. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp. If using frozen vegetables, make sure they're thawed and well-drained first.
  • Add the rice: Push the vegetables to the sides of the wok and add the remaining tablespoon of oil to the center. Add the cold rice, breaking up any remaining clumps. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, tossing and flipping constantly, until the rice is heated through and starting to get slightly crispy in spots.
  • Season the rice: Drizzle the soy sauce and oyster sauce over the rice. Add the white pepper. Toss everything together vigorously, making sure the sauces are evenly distributed and the rice is well-coated. The rice should take on a light brown color from the soy sauce.
  • Combine everything: Add the scrambled eggs back to the wok, breaking them into smaller pieces as you toss. Add the green parts of the green onions and toss everything together for another minute.
  • Finish: Remove from heat and drizzle with sesame oil. Toss once more to distribute the sesame oil, which adds a wonderful nutty aroma and flavor. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional soy sauce or salt if needed.
  • Serve: Serve immediately while hot. Fried rice is delicious on its own or as a side dish to your favorite Asian-inspired meals.

TIP: The key to restaurant quality fried rice is high heat and constant movement. Don't be afraid to really crank up the heat, you want that slightly smoky, charred flavor (called "wok hei"). Use a large enough pan so the rice isn't crowded and can get crispy. You can customize this recipe endlessly—add protein, different vegetables, or try it with pineapple for a Hawaiian twist.
