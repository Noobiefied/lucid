Chicken Stir Fry

Start your morning right with a stack of these cloud-like pancakes. They're so simple to make from scratch, you'll never reach for a boxed mix again. Top with your favorite fruits, syrup, or whipped cream.

Source: Gordan Ramsey Recipes

Ingredients

Ingredients for chicken stir fries are as follows:

For the chicken marinade:

  • 1½ pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine or dry sherry
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

For the vegetables:

  • 2 bell peppers (any color), cut into strips
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas or snow peas
  • 1 medium carrot, thinly sliced

For the sauce:

  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or honey
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • ¼ cup chicken broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

For cooking:

  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Optional: red pepper flakes or sriracha for heat

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Marinate the chicken: In a medium bowl, combine the chicken strips with 2 tablespoons soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, 1 tablespoon cornstarch, and 1 teaspoon sesame oil. Mix well to coat all the chicken pieces. Let it marinate for at least 15 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients. This tenderizes the chicken and adds flavor.
  • Prepare the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, cornstarch, chicken broth, and sesame oil. Set aside. Having your sauce ready before you start cooking is essential for stir-frying, as things move quickly once you start.
  • Prep the vegetables: Cut all your vegetables and have them ready near the stove. In stir-frying, the cooking happens fast, so you need everything prepared and within arm's reach.
  • Cook the chiken: Heat a large wok or skillet over high heat until very hot. Add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil and swirl to coat. Add the marinated chicken in a single layer (you may need to do this in batches to avoid overcrowding). Let it sear for about 2 minutes without moving it, then stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and lightly browned. Transfer the chicken to a clean plate and set aside.
  • Stir-fry the aromatics: Add the remaining tablespoon of oil to the wok. Add the minced garlic and ginger and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  • Cook the vegetables: Add the harder vegetables first (broccoli and carrots) and stir-fry for 2 minutes. Then add the bell peppers, onion, and snap peas. Continue stir-frying for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender. They should still have a bit of crunch.
  • Combine and sauce: Return the chicken to the wok with the vegetables. Give the sauce a quick stir (the cornstarch settles), then pour it over the chicken and vegetables.
  • Finish: Toss everything together for about 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats everything evenly. The sauce should be glossy and cling to the chicken and vegetables.
  • Garnish and Serve: Remove from heat, sprinkle with sliced green onions, and serve immediately over steamed rice or noodles.

TIP: The secret to a great stir-fry is high heat and quick cooking. Your wok or pan should be smoking hot. Cook in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding, which causes steaming instead of searing. You can swap the vegetables for whatever you have on hand—mushrooms, zucchini, baby corn, and water chestnuts all work great.
