Fluffy Pancakes

Start your morning right with a stack of these cloud-like pancakes. They're so simple to make from scratch, you'll never reach for a boxed mix again. Top with your favorite fruits, syrup, or whipped cream.

Source: Easy recipes2025

Ingredients

Ingredients for fluffy pancakes are as follows:

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1¼ cups whole milk, room temperature
  • 1 large egg, room temperature
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Butter or oil for cooking

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Combine dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar until well combined and no lumps remain.
  • Mix wet ingredients: In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth and well combined.
  • Combine wet and dry: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Using a whisk or wooden spoon, gently stir until just combined. The batter should be slightly lumpy—do not overmix! Overmixing will result in tough, dense pancakes. A few small lumps are perfectly fine and will disappear during cooking.
  • Rest the batter: Let the batter rest for 5-10 minutes while you heat your griddle or pan. This allows the baking powder to activate and the gluten to relax, resulting in fluffier pancakes.
  • Heat your cooking surface: Heat a griddle or large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add a small amount of butter or spray with cooking oil. The pan is ready when a drop of water sizzles and evaporates quickly.
  • Cook the pancakes: Pour about ¼ cup of batter onto the griddle for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2-3 minutes. When the bubbles begin to pop and the bottom is golden brown, flip the pancake.
  • Finish: Cook for another 1-2 minutes on the second side until golden brown and cooked through. The pancake should spring back lightly when touched.
  • Keep warn: Transfer cooked pancakes to a plate and cover with a clean kitchen towel to keep warm while you cook the remaining batter. You can also keep them warm in a 200°F oven.
  • Serve: Stack the pancakes and serve immediately with butter, maple syrup, fresh berries, whipped cream, or your favorite toppings.

TIP: For extra fluffy pancakes, separate the egg and beat the white until soft peaks form, then fold it into the batter at the end. Don't press down on pancakes while cooking—this deflates them. For consistent sizes, use a measuring cup or ice cream scoop.
