Garlic Butter Shrimp

Succulent shrimp cooked in fragrant garlic butter with a hint of lemon. This elegant dish comes together in under 15 minutes, making it perfect for busy weeknights or impressive dinner parties.

Source: TeeYvette

Ingredients

Ingredients for garlic butter shrimp are as follows:

  • 1½ pounds large shrimp (about 24-30), peeled and deveined, tails on or off
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • ¼ cup dry white wine or chicken broth (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lemon (about 2 tablespoons)
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Prepare the shrimp: Pat the shrimp completely dry with paper towels. This is important for getting a good sear. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  • Heat the pan: In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of butter and the olive oil over medium-high heat until the butter is melted and the pan is hot but not smoking.
  • Sear the shrimp: Add the shrimp in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan (work in batches if necessary). Cook for about 2 minutes on the first side without moving them, until they're pink and slightly golden.
  • Flip and finish: Flip the shrimp and cook for another 1-2 minutes until the other side is pink and the shrimp are just cooked through. They should be opaque and slightly curled. Be careful not to overcook—shrimp cook very quickly and become rubbery if overdone. Transfer the cooked shrimp to a plate.
  • Make the garlic butter sauce: Reduce the heat to medium and add the remaining 3 tablespoons of butter to the same skillet. Once melted, add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes if using. Sauté for about 30-60 seconds, stirring constantly, until the garlic is fragrant but not browned.
  • Deglaze(optional): If using wine or broth, pour it into the pan and let it simmer for about 1 minute, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. This adds extra flavor to your sauce.
  • Finish the sauce: Add the lemon juice and stir to combine. Let the sauce simmer for another minute to reduce slightly and concentrate the flavors.
  • Combine and serve: Return the shrimp to the skillet and toss them in the garlic butter sauce until well coated and heated through, about 1 minute. Sprinkle with fresh chopped parsley and toss again.
  • Plate and garnish: Transfer to a serving dish and spoon any remaining sauce over the top. Serve immediately with lemon wedges on the side.

TIP: Serve over pasta, rice, or with crusty bread to soak up the delicious garlic butter sauce. You can also add a splash of heavy cream to make it extra decadent. For best results, use fresh (not frozen) shrimp if possible, and don't skip the step of patting them dry.
