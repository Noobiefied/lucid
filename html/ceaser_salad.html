Classic Ceaser Salad Dressing

Rich, garlicky, and authentically delicious, this from-scratch dressing transforms ordinary romaine into a memorable salad. Once you try homemade, you'll never go back to bottled.

Source: Mason Recipes

Ingredients

Ingredients for classic ceaser salad are as follows:

  • 2 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
  • 2-3 anchovy fillets (or 1 teaspoon anchovy paste)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about ½ lemon)
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 large egg yolk (see note for safety)
  • ½ cup extra virgin olive oil
  • ⅓ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste (be careful, anchovies and Parmesan are salty)

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Prepare your base: In a food processor or blender, combine the garlic and anchovy fillets. Pulse several times until they form a paste. If you don't have a food processor, you can mince the garlic very finely and mash it with the anchovies using the back of a fork on a cutting board with a pinch of salt.
  • Add acidic ingredients: Add the lemon juice, Dijon mustard, and egg yolk to the food processor. The egg yolk acts as an emulsifier, helping to create a creamy, stable dressing.
  • Blend: Process or blend for about 30 seconds until the mixture is smooth and well combined.
  • Emulsify with oil: With the food processor or blender running on low speed, very slowly drizzle in the olive oil through the feed tube. Start with just a few drops, then increase to a thin, steady stream as the mixture begins to emulsify. This gradual process is crucial for creating a thick, creamy dressing. The whole process should take about 2-3 minutes.
  • Add cheese and seasonings: Once all the oil is incorporated and the dressing is thick and creamy, add the grated Parmesan cheese, Worcestershire sauce, and black pepper. Pulse a few times until combined.
  • Adjust consistency and seasoning: Taste the dressing and add salt if needed (remember, it's probably already quite salty from the anchovies and Parmesan). If the dressing is too thick, you can thin it with a tablespoon of water or additional lemon juice. If it's too thin, you can add a bit more Parmesan.
  • Store or serve: Transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate until ready to use. The dressing will thicken slightly when chilled. It will keep for up to 3-4 days in the refrigerator.
  • Toss the salad: When ready to serve, toss the dressing with crisp romaine lettuce, additional Parmesan shavings, and croutons for a classic Caesar salad.

Safety Note: This recipe contains raw egg yolk. To reduce the risk of foodborne illness, use pasteurized eggs, or you can substitute with ¼ cup of mayonnaise (which is made from pasteurized eggs) instead of the egg yolk for a safer version.

TIP: For the best flavor, use good quality Parmesan cheese and freshly squeezed lemon juice. Don't skip the anchovies—they provide essential umami flavor without making the dressing taste fishy. If you're really opposed to anchovies, you can increase the Worcestershire sauce, but the authentic flavor will be missing.
