Basic Guacamole

Chunky or smooth, spicy or mild, this guacamole recipe is your blank canvas. Fresh avocados, zesty lime, and a kick of jalapeño make this addictive dip disappear fast at any gathering.

Source: Sarah Foster

Ingredients

Ingredients for guacamole are as follows:

  • 3 ripe avocados
  • 1 lime, juiced (about 2 tablespoons)
  • ½ teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • ½ cup diced white or red onion
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 Roma tomatoes, seeded and diced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced (adjust to taste)
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cumin (optional)
  • ⅛ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Prepare the avocados: Cut the avocados in half lengthwise and remove the pits. Use a spoon to scoop the flesh into a medium mixing bowl.
  • Mash the avocados: Add the lime juice and salt to the avocados. Using a fork or potato masher, mash the avocados to your desired consistency. Some people prefer it chunky with visible pieces of avocado, while others like it smooth and creamy. I recommend leaving it slightly chunky for the best texture.
  • Add the aromatics: Add the diced onion, chopped cilantro, and minced jalapeño to the mashed avocado. If you're sensitive to heat, start with half the jalapeño and taste before adding more. You can also leave the seeds in if you want it spicier.
  • Fold in tomatoes: Gently fold in the diced tomatoes. Be careful not to overmix at this point, as you want the tomatoes to maintain their shape and not make the guacamole watery.
  • Add optional seasonings: If using, stir in the cumin, cayenne pepper, and minced garlic. These additions add depth and complexity to the flavor.
  • Taste and adjust: Taste your guacamole and adjust the seasoning. You may want to add more salt, lime juice, or jalapeño depending on your preference. Remember that the flavors will meld together as it sits.
  • Serve Immediately: Guacamole is best served fresh. Transfer to a serving bowl and serve with tortilla chips, vegetable sticks, or as a topping for tacos, burritos, or nachos.
  • Storage Tip: If you need to store it, press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the guacamole to prevent browning, then refrigerate for up to 1 day. The lime juice helps prevent oxidation, but guacamole will naturally darken over time.

TIP: Choose ripe avocados that yield to gentle pressure but aren't mushy. To ripen avocados faster, place them in a paper bag with a banana. For a smoother guacamole, you can pulse everything in a food processor, but traditional guacamole is always made by hand.
