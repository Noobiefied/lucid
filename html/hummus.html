Homemade Hummus

Silky, garlicky, and ridiculously easy to make in a blender. This protein-packed dip costs a fraction of store-bought and tastes infinitely better. Customize it with roasted red peppers, herbs, or extra lemon.

Source: Pintrest

Ingredients

Ingredients for homemade hummus are as follows:

  • 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas (garbanzo beans), drained and rinsed
  • ¼ cup tahini (sesame seed paste), well-stirred
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, roughly chopped
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 1 lemon)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ¾ teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 2-3 tablespoons cold water (to adjust consistency)
  • Paprika, olive oil, and fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Here are the instructions to prepare this delacacy:

  • Optional step for ultra-smooth hummus: For the creamiest possible hummus, remove the skins from the chickpeas. This is tedious but makes a noticeable difference. After draining and rinsing, gently rub the chickpeas between your hands or in a kitchen towel. The skins will slip off easily. You don't need to get every single one—about 75% is fine.
  • Process the tahini and lemon: In a food processor, combine the tahini and lemon juice. Process for about 1 minute, scraping down the sides as needed. The mixture will initially look grainy and separated, but it will become smooth and creamy as it processes. This step is important for developing the texture.
  • Add the aromatics: Add the garlic, olive oil, cumin, and salt to the tahini mixture. Process for another 30 seconds until well combined.
  • Add the chickpeas: Add the chickpeas (peeled or not) to the food processor. Process for 1-2 minutes, stopping to scrape down the sides as needed. The mixture will be thick and somewhat crumbly at first.
  • Adjust consistency: With the processor running, slowly drizzle in the cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the hummus reaches your desired consistency. You want it smooth and creamy but still thick enough to hold its shape. Some people like it thick for scooping, others prefer it thinner for spreading. Add water until it's just right for you.
  • Process until smooth: Continue processing for another 1-2 minutes until the hummus is very smooth and creamy. Taste and adjust seasoning, adding more salt, lemon juice, or garlic as desired.
  • Serve or chill: Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl. If serving immediately, use the back of a spoon to create a well in the center. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with paprika and fresh chopped parsley. You can also add pine nuts, olives, or roasted red peppers as garnish.
  • Storage: Cover and refrigerate if not serving right away. Hummus will keep for up to 5-7 days in an airtight container in the refrigerator. The flavors actually develop and improve after a day. Let it come to room temperature before serving for the best flavor and texture.

TIP: Quality tahini makes a huge difference—look for brands that are smooth and creamy, not separated or bitter. If your hummus tastes bland, it probably needs more salt or lemon juice. For variations, try adding roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, roasted garlic, or fresh herbs like basil or cilantro.
